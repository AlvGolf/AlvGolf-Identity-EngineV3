The Sand Wedge (-5.5°) is the only club showing truly optimal descending mechanics, suggesting Álvaro *can* produce the correct motion but hasn't yet automated it across the full set.

## 4. ROOT CAUSE IDENTIFICATION

**Primary Biomechanical Issue: Universal Shallow Transition**

The root cause connecting all observed patterns is a **shallow downswing transition** that produces ascending or near-flat attack angles across the bag. This single mechanical fault manifests differently by club:

- **With Driver:** Produces +4.1° (slightly excessive ascending) → excess spin, left-biased dispersion
- **With Fairway Woods/Hybrid:** Produces +3.3°/+0.5° (ascending when should be descending) → **critical distance loss**
- **With Mid-Irons:** Produces -1.3° to -2.5° (too shallow) → inconsistent compression, high launch
- **With Wedges:** Partially corrected by steeper setup → explains why SW is the best-performing club mechanically

**Why does this happen?** The data shows Álvaro's attack angle *evolved* from +7.3° (June 2024) to +4.2° (March 2025) to +0.8° (July 2025) to +2.3° (December 2025). He has been **actively working on reducing his ascending blow** — but the correction has been applied globally, making his irons shallower when they should be getting steeper. He's solving the wrong problem with the driver while creating a new problem with irons.

**Secondary Issue: Face Angle Instability at Impact**

The PW consistency score of 14/100 and SW distance CV of 18.9% indicate **face angle variance** at impact, not path issues. This is a release timing problem — the hands are releasing at inconsistent points through the impact zone, producing both distance and directional scatter.

**Tertiary Issue: 5-Iron/6-Iron Setup Inconsistency**

The complete reversal of directional bias between 5-iron (100% right) and 6-iron (63% left) indicates a **ball position or alignment change** between these clubs that is creating two entirely different swing paths. This is likely a setup habit, not a swing fault.

**Interdependencies:**
- Fixing shallow transition → improves iron attack angle → improves launch angle → reduces spin → improves distance
- Fixing shallow transition → improves 3-wood attack angle → **immediate +12-15m gain**
- Fixing face stability → improves PW/SW consistency scores → directly reduces double-bogey rate (currently 53.3%)

**Equipment Consideration:**
The 3-Wood/Hybrid gap of 41.8m is flagged in the data. However, before adding equipment, fixing the 3-wood attack angle (+3.3° → -1°) would recover 12-15m, reducing the gap to ~27m. Equipment solution should follow mechanical correction.

## 5. CORRECTIVE ACTION PLAN

### PRIORITY #1: Iron Attack Angle — Descending Blow Drill
**Target:** Move 7-iron attack angle from -1.3° to -4° to -5°
**Timeline:** 6-8 weeks to groove

**Drill — "Forward Divot Gate":**
Place two tees in the ground: one at ball position, one 10cm forward (toward target). The divot must start AT the ball tee and extend PAST the forward tee. No divot before the ball = scooping. 
- **Execution:** 20 iron shots daily (7-iron focus), half-speed first week
- **Feel cue:** "Hands reach the forward tee before the club reaches the ball"
- **Check:** Divot depth should be 2-3cm, starting at ball, extending 8-10cm forward

**Expected improvement:** Attack angle -1.3° → -3.5° in 4 weeks, -4° to -5° in 8 weeks. **Distance gain: +8-12m with 7-iron** (better compression). Launch angle drops from 21.9° to 19-20° (optimal). Consistency score improvement: 41 → 55+.

---

### PRIORITY #2: 3-Wood Attack Angle — Descending Fairway Wood Drill
**Target:** Move 3-wood attack angle from +3.3° to -1° to 0°
**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks (highest ROI drill in the bag)

**Drill — "Ball Back, Hands Forward":**
Move ball position 1 ball back from normal 3-wood position (just inside left heel → center-left). Place alignment stick on ground pointing at target. At address, hands should be 2-3cm ahead of ball. Feel: "compress the ball into the ground, not sweep it."
- **Execution:** 15 shots per session, 3 sessions per week
- **Feel cue:** "Hit down on the 3-wood like a long iron"
- **Check:** Should see a small divot or turf interaction after the ball

**Expected improvement:** Attack angle +3.3° → 0° to -1°. **Immediate distance gain: +12-15m per 3-wood shot** (202m → 215-217m average). This single change is worth approximately **0.5-0.8 strokes per round** given how frequently fairway woods are used from the tee and fairway.

---

### PRIORITY #3: PW/SW Face Stability — Impact Bag Drill
**Target:** Reduce PW distance CV from 14.5% to <8%, SW from 18.9% to <10%
**Timeline:** 3-4 weeks

**Drill — "Frozen Hands Through Impact":**
Using an impact bag or folded towel, make half-swing wedge shots focusing on maintaining the face angle through impact. The right hand should NOT roll over the left until 30cm past impact.
- **Execution:** 25 reps daily, alternating PW and SW
- **Feel cue:** "Logo of glove faces sky at follow-through"
- **Check:** Ball should fly straight with consistent height

**Expected improvement:** PW consistency score 14 → 35+, SW distance CV 18.9% → 12%. **Scoring impact: reduces double-bogey rate** (currently 53.3%) by stabilizing the scoring zone clubs.
